''Contemporary archaeological sciences research in Egypt and the Greek contribution''

ANNOUNCEMENT

The newly established Greek archaeological scientific mission in Alexandria, Egypt, with its Archaeological Sciences Institute of the Aegean at Alexandria (ASIAA), holds the first scientific symposium in Alexandria; a one-day meeting, on 11th of October at the Amphitheatre of the Greek Community at Chatby.

The Symposium is funded by the Interreg II Program and it is under the aegis of the Dept. of Mediterranean Studies, University of the Aegean, Rhodes, the founder of ASIAA.

The Symposium's theme session is:

''Contemporary archaeological sciences research in Egypt and the Greek contribution''

Presentations will be in English, duration of each lecture will be 20mins each, slide and overhead projectors will be available. Proceedings will be published in the Mediterranean Archaeology & Archaeometry Journal, provided that they follow the strict refereeing procedure of the Journal (web site: www.rhodes.aegean.gr/maa_journal).

We envisage about twenty presentations, but at the moment there is room for potential participants to apply. We need an abstract of one page, including title of presentation, name and full address, telephone and e-mail, send to Prof. Ioannis Liritzis, preferably via e-mail: liritzis@rhodes.aegean.gr.

The local travel and maintenance expenses are all covered by the organizing committee (OC).
